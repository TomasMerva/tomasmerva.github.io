I am PhD student at the Department of Industrial Automation and Mechatronics at Technical University of Kosice, Slovakia.


I have received my BSc and MSc degrees from the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Informatics, Technical University of Kosice, both in electrical drives (control and construction of electric motors, power electronics, microprocessors). My research interest within my PhD studies is focused on the optimization-based integrated Task and Motion Planning (TAMP) under the supervision of Ivan VIRGALA. Before starting my PhD journey, I was a junior technician at the Department of Electrical Engineering and Mechatronics, where I programmed battery cells testers for analyzing charging and discharging effects.

Research

Overview

My current research focuses on sequential robotic manipulation (TAMP), in which a robot needs to autonomously find out the sequence of actions that needs to perform in order to achieve the desired goal. The aim of my PhD studies is developing methods for reactive execution of such sequences.

My main application domain is robotic manipulators.
